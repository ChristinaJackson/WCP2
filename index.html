    • Water Crisis Project

    102,434 residents do not have clean water.

    Our mission:

    The Water Crisis Project puts the power back into people's hands by teaching people how to purify their own water and providing the resources necessary to do so.

    We hope to help as many families as possible and especially those who are immobile and unable get to clean water sources.

    The people of Flint are not only suffering from the devastation from the water crisis but they have the highest water bills. The city has been shutting off residents' water for not paying for poisoned water.

    The problem with bottled water:

    • It's the most effective way to purify water and eliminates the use of plastic water bottles.
    • 25% or more of the water used can come from the tap, samples can contain phthalates, mold, microbes, benzene, trihalomethanes, even arsenic.
    • Plastic bottles are not recycled, most of the time. The plastic can end up in our oceans and water sources, contaminating them as well.

    We provide:

    • Countertop distillers for personal, in-home use.
    • The convenience of having clean water in your own home, especially for those that cannot travel to clean water.
    • Removes contaminants from water including heavy metals like lead and copper.
    • Distills from 4 -8 gallons of pure, clean drinking water a day (depending on the size of the distiller).

    Our work in Flint:

    About Us:

    I'm Louis Williams. Long ago, sometime around the 1980s, a friend of mine was studying civil engineering and we were discussing how water infrastructure in most American cities have old systems. The pipe were laid in early century and were made of lead. We knew then that lead was dangerous to ingest at any level.

    I started to buy 5 gallon bottles of filtered water, but it did not take me long to figure out with human error I was still taking a risk by purchasing filtered water as I learned most filters failed to clean water efficiently.

    It made sense to me that I can feel safe about distilled water that I processed myself because I'm the only one who can make sure of what I'm drinking is safe. Also, having the convenience of not lugging five gallon bottles is great!

    My niece, Anura, and I saw this was another idea that would aid the folks in Flint to have a little more control of their lives. We want people to know that we are still watching and we care. We have not forgotten about Flint.

    Keep goin' Flint,
    Louis and Anura
